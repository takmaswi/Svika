# Roadmap — Svika

The features below are out of scope for the hackathon submission. They appear in the pitch deck and the README under "Roadmap." None of them are in code.

The roadmap is grouped by the value problem each phase addresses, not by calendar dates.

## Phase Six — Accessibility and reach

**Why:** the smartphone-only assumption excludes a large share of Zimbabweans. The system has to meet the carrier-menu user where they are.

- **Carrier-menu access (the *123# style).** Balance check, trip plan, ticket transfer, top-up — all available without data, on any phone. Requires a relationship with a mobile network operator and a USSD aggregator.
- **Voice-first entry over the carrier menu.** A short interactive voice response flow for users who prefer speaking to typing on a numeric keypad.
- **Multi-language support beyond Shona and English.** Ndebele as the next priority for southern routes.

## Phase Seven — Real money

**Why:** the demo uses pre-loaded credit balances. The real product needs real top-ups and real settlement.

- **EcoCash top-up integration.** Through Paynow's existing EcoCash gateway, with proper merchant onboarding.
- **Innbucks top-up integration.** Second mobile money option to avoid single-rail risk.
- **Conductor cash-out.** Daily settlement of digital fares to the kombi's wallet, then to the fleet owner's bank or mobile money account.
- **Cash-out via "rank merchant" partners.** A network of nearby vendors who exchange digital balance for physical cash for users who need it.

## Phase Eight — Trust and safety

**Why:** the kombi industry has real safety problems. The system can help.

- **Three-strike speeding engine.** PostGIS-based velocity calculation between consecutive kombi pings. Audio warnings for the conductor on first strike, owner alert on second, transport authority alert on third.
- **Emergency medical manifest (ICE).** A dynamic page generated by detected impact or a panic button, listing the emergency contacts and medical-aid details of every passenger boarded on the kombi at that moment. Sent to MARS or EMRAS dispatch by webhook.
- **Driver and conductor identity verification.** Onboarding photo, registered phone, name on the kombi sticker. Public profile visible to passengers.
- **Compliance score per vehicle.** Visible to fleet owners and to the city. Affects which routes the kombi can serve.

## Phase Nine — The full trip planner

**Why:** the demo planner reads pre-computed plans. The real product needs to plan any origin-destination pair.

- **Graph-based trip planner.** A real shortest-path algorithm over the route graph plus walking edges between stop points. Supports willingness-to-walk preferences and time-of-day reliability scoring.
- **Multi-stop routing with dwell-time penalties.** When a kombi is over 80% full, the planner penalises that segment to reflect the wait time at the stop.
- **Live arrival prediction from real telemetry.** Replace the simulation runner with a feed from the actual conductor screens (their phones become the GPS source).
- **Route changes detection.** When a kombi deviates from its registered polyline, mark it for review.

## Phase Ten — Two-sided commerce

**Why:** the kombi network is already a logistics network. Formalising it opens revenue for everyone in it.

- **Two-code parcel handover.** A handover code for the conductor and a release code for the receiver. Prevents fake delivery claims.
- **Same-day commercial last-mile delivery.** Local couriers, fast-food restaurants, or e-commerce stores plug into the network as senders.
- **Sender insurance and tiered limits.** Ride-and-deliver carries different liability than a passenger fare.
- **Anti-fraud detection on parcels.** Flag suspicious patterns — high-value parcels at unusual hours, repeated cancellations, contraband indicators.

## Phase Eleven — Data products

**Why:** the data Svika generates is uniquely valuable. Treat it as a product.

- **Urban mobility dashboard for city planners.** Aggregated, anonymised PostGIS data showing real flows by hour, by route, by stop. Subscription access for planning departments and consultancies.
- **ZIMRA tax integration.** Direct API access for the Zimbabwe Revenue Authority into the per-fleet revenue ledger. Compliance certificates auto-generated. Operators receive tax credits for participation.
- **Route-verification automation.** The product itself uses Google Maps and Waze APIs to verify route polylines on a schedule, flagging changes and proposing updates to operators. The user observed this in the build phase — it belongs here.
- **Open APIs for transit researchers.** Read-only, anonymised, cited.

## Phase Twelve — Real partner integrations

**Why:** the demo references partners as design intent. The real product activates the relationships.

- **ZUPCO ticket interoperability.** Svika tickets are honoured on ZUPCO buses and vice versa. Joint ticket denominations.
- **WhatsApp Business API integration.** Replace the `/wa` mock with a real Meta-approved WhatsApp Business presence.
- **Real-time bank settlement.** For the formal operators, daily wire of net revenue to corporate accounts.
- **Insurance partnerships.** Per-trip micro-insurance bundled into the fare.

## Out of roadmap

The following are explicitly not on the roadmap and will not be built:

- A native mobile application. The web is the right surface for the foreseeable future.
- A subscription tier for passengers. The escrow and per-segment fare margins are sufficient.
- Advertising on passenger screens. Compromises trust, attracts regulatory attention.
- Cryptocurrency, tokens, or any blockchain element. Solves no real Svika problem.
- Surge pricing. The kombi network already prices dynamically through fuel-driven fare updates; algorithmic surge would attract scrutiny without adding value.
